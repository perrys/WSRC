Occaisionally we have a problem with condensation appearing on the court walls (sometimes called “sweating” walls), particularly later in the evening when the club is busy. When this occurs it can render the court unusable for competitive matches.

In general, condensation occurs when moist air comes into contact with a cool surface. The more humid the air, the less cold the surface needs to be for condensation to form on it.

We are gathering temperature and humidity data to try to understand the cause (or causes) of the problem, in order to decide how best to tackle it. We would be very grateful to members if they could report any occurences of condensation on the courts using the form below, so these can be compared to the temperature and humidity measurements when the problem occurs.
